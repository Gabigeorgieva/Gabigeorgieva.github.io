Gabriela's Design Document

Purpose

This website will serve as a portfolio to showcase my design skills and various artwork/digital design I have done throughout my years so that I can have something to show recruiters when I graduate and am looking for a job. It will provide a space for recruiters to learn more about me, see my skillset and decide if I seem like a good fit for their company. If they decide I am, it will have my contact information so that they can easily reach out to me and start a dialogue which could hopefully lead to a job offer. It would help show the employers clearly and quickly whether I had the skills needed for the job they are looking to hire for. It would also provide a space for others outside the work field to contact me if they are looking to hire design help because I am a freelance artist on the side. This would not only help get me a job but also build me into looking like a true professional on my own. Most importantly though, by looking at my portfolio the employers would gain the knowledge they needed that would help determine if I was an adequate candidate for the position they were wanting to fill.

Use Cases

Use 1: Find Employee

On Monday, the University of Missouri recruiter will report back to work on to find out they need to fill a position on their web design team. He looks posts the job and applications start quickly coming in. He looks through each applicant and rules out the ones without a portfolio. He then starts looking at each portfolio, eventually coming to mine. He would land on the home page which tells him who I am and what my specialties are. He can then scroll down and the first thing he will see is a little copy about me and an image link to my portfolio page. He can scroll more and there will list specific options on my portfolio page such as Web Design, Logos, Advertising and Artwork. Each section would have a link image to take him to the specific page that showcases that part of my portfolio. He clicks on the Web Design image link since that is what interests him most since that is what he is hiring for. He will be taken to the page where he can scroll through images of each web design project I have showcased and can read more about each project by clicking on the image he wants to know more about, and it'll take him to another page that only showcases that specific website. He can scroll through and see the images of the site showcased and read the copy that compliments it. In the end, there will be a link to the actual web site so that he can visit it himself if he chooses to. He decides he's seen enough from being on my site and that I have the skills needed so he goes to the contact page on the top navigation bar and finds my information. He then sends me an email to set up an interview.

Use 2: Find Artist

On Saturday, a soon to be mom is decorating her baby's bedroom. She decides she needs to find some paintings for the walls because they feel bare. She doesn't want a typical picture that anyone could buy from the store, she wants something original and sentimental made specifically for her new baby. She goes online and googles artist in Columbia and my website comes up. She clicks on it and is taken to the home page. She would land on the home page which tells her who I am and what my specialties are. She can then scroll down and the first thing she will see is a little copy about me and an image link to my portfolio page. She can scroll more and there will list specific options from my portfolio pages such as Web Design, Logos, Advertising and Artwork. Each section would have a link image to take her to the specific page that showcases that part of my portfolio. She clicks on the basic Portfolio page because she decides she wants to see everything I do before deciding on what she wants. She is taken to the portfolio page that again lists the portfolio options by showing the main image for each page option. She decides artwork is the most applicable to what she is looking for, so she clicks on the artwork image and is taken to the artwork page. There it will showcase images of some of my favorite piece of art that she can scroll through and look at. She decides I have the skills needed to make her baby's custom painting and wants to reach out. She goes to the contact page on the top navigation bar and finds my information. She then sends me an email to set up a consultation and ask about pricing.

On Tuesday, a new café shop is about to open up in Columbia, Missouri. The owner needs to get a logo made for the shop but doesn't have a lot of money to spend. She goes online and googles graphic designer in Columbia hoping to find something cheaper than if she hired a big company to do it for her and my website comes up. She clicks on it and is taken to the home page. She would land on the home page which tells her who I am and what my specialties are. She can then scroll down and the first thing she will see is a little copy about me and an image link to my portfolio page. She can scroll more and there will list specific options from my portfolio pages such as Web Design, Logos, Advertising and Artwork. Each section would have a link image to take her to the specific page that showcases that part of my portfolio. She clicks on the logo page because she decides she wants to only see what is applicable to what her business needs. She is taken to the logo page where it will showcase images of some of my favorite logos that I have done for clients that she can scroll through and look at. She decides I have the skills needed to make her café's new logo and wants to reach out. She goes to the contact page on the top navigation bar and finds my information. She then sends me an email to set up a consultation regarding the logo and to ask about pricing.

Audience

The target audience of this webpage will be recruiters in Columbia, Missouri looking to hire and fill design positions at their company. They can be male or female and are usually in the 25-40 age range. For a living, they are recruiters for their company and they love their job because they get to meet new creative minds every day. Hobbies include spending time with family and doing outdoor activities in Columbia. Primarily this website will be ideal for recruiters who have a computer and internet access at work. A printer would not be needed because nothing would need to be printed from the website. I will send in the link to my website with my resume whenever I apply for a position. They would not have any prior knowledge about me, but they would know their company and what their company is looking for, so they would be looking to hire someone that would be the best fit.

Below is the list of website resources utilized as good and bad examples of design:

  • https://joshmiller.com.au/work/kojai

    Josh Miller's is a really good website example to me because it's not only beautiful, but it has very clear Navigation at the top and the color scheme is fun and used very well throughout the whole site. If a recruiter went on his site they would be able to easily see his skill set and contact him.

  • https://www.alexcoven.com

    Alex Coven's website is a really good example for me. I like how the navigation is hidden on the side, but I worry that it could be hard to find for some people because it is not clearly labeled. If a recruiter was on his site, they would see his skill set but may have trouble finding how to contact him because it's in the hidden side navigation. If it's too difficult they may decide it's not worth it and not reach out.

  • https://www.annaxphotography.com/

    Anna's website is a photography portfolio. I like how she uses her photos as the main content on the page because that shows that they are of most importance. I personally like seeing a little more color, I think she could have utilized the beautiful color from her header throughout the whole site but keeping everything else white does keep the site looking very clean. If someone was looking for a photographer instead of art like use case 2 they would clearly be able to see if her style matched with theirs and easily find her contact information to further the dialogue.

  • http://www.rleonardi.com/

    This website was very impressive with its animation and style. The artist is clearly very talented, and it was interactive and fun to scroll through. That being said there is no quick way to see everything and everything is very busy so it's hard to know what to look at in my opinion. I wish some parts could have been a little more simplified or had an option at the end that shows everything without having to scroll through the whole website to find what you're looking for. A recruiter would still probably be very impressed and there is easy contact navigation at the top but if they were looking for something specific it could be a little frustrating or overwhelmed with everything on the site.

  • https://www.danielspatzek.com/viita_titan

    I think this site would be a bad example for me. The text is cluttered and hard to read and when you scroll there is a big paragraph that just follows you, but you can't read it because the images it scrolls over takes away the readability. The interaction is nice, but I just don't think it was done with ease of reading intention which is important to a website portfolio. The color scheme and is constant which is good but because of all the cluttered text, if someone was looking for a logo like in use case 3, they probably wouldn't know where to look and you would lose their business.

Needs Assessment

In determining whether a portfolio website would be helpful for the intended audience, a survey was administered four individuals based on my target audience were interviewed on the topic. All four of the individual work in the web design field and two of which have the ability to hire people.

Interview Questions:

Why would a portfolio website be important to you if hiring?

Responses included:

  • Crucial in the hiring process for web or logo design.
  • Allows a potential employer to review substantial works and evidence of the applicant's qualifications.
  • Shows off your creativity as a designer and ability to make clean readable code.
  • Shows what the individual's skill set level was in their portfolio.
  • It would help determine what their creative process is Show how recent their work is.

How often would you use such information?

Responses included:

  • It would be reviewed for every applicant if it were available to view.
  • This would help determine if the person was right for the job or not.

What specific content would you be most interested in?

Responses included:

  • The websites that an applicant has made to ensure they have a solid grasp of the website design process as well as meeting the client's expectations.
  • If it comes to coding, show what languages and parts of applications you worked on.
  • Variety in the work.
  • Understanding current trends in web design.
  • The person's use of color and typography.
  • If it was responsive or not.
  • If information is spaced out evenly.
  • Show content that's related to the present web/graphics market.

What would stand out to you in a portfolio website?

Responses included:

  • A responsive website that is viewable on various devices and viewports would stand out the most.
  • Design as a coder would make you stand out, coders aren't good at communication or design, and being a female coder that can do all that would make you stand out since it is a male-dominated field.
  • A portfolio that showed the applicants ability to be flexible and adapt to different ideas/concepts to create a modern website.
  • How organized the website is.
  • The use of content – spacing, fonts, colors – do they all work together?
  • Photos – Did the person use their own images
  • Subject Matter – Did the subject matter work with or against the design or message the person was going for.

The rationale of Organization

Design elements and colors will be consistent throughout all pages. Color choices will represent the colors of my brand. Navigation buttons will be on the top of every page for easy access. A link to the home page will also be provided at the bottom of each page or a back to top button on the side of the bottom so they don't have to scroll a lot if they don't want to. Having the portfolio divided the way I did makes the information easier to consume for the viewer. Since I do a lot of different things, if I simply lumped everything together it would look very disorganized and the viewer would have a hard time finding exactly what they were looking for.

Media

I will use my own images and graphic design when possible. If not possible, I will use images that are free for use on Pixels.com or Unsplash.com. These sites don't require you to cite your source but if I needed to I would on the link or in the footer. Pictures will be used to a link to a lot of sections and then also to showcase all my work. It will also help make things more visually appealing to the viewer, since this is a portfolio website, visual appeal is very important. Pictures of teachers will be taken by myself.

Color Scheme and Rationale

Recently I have made some business card that portray what I want my brand to be pretty well. I made my resume to watch visually and color wise, so I would want my colors for my website to be the same as that so that everything goes together nicely. My background color will be either white, a light grey or purple/blue color, the header color will be mint green or white and the buttons (if any, will be the purple/blue). I plan to use these greys, purple/blues, mint greens, and whites throughout the whole site for consistency and visual appeal.
